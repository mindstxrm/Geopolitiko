Getting started

Geopolitical Terminal provides a Situation Room (global map), Intelligence Feed, Risk Engine, country dashboards, Scenario Engine, and Workspace for multi-panel views.

  • Start: Situation Room — map layers (Political Stability, Conflict, Sanctions, Trade, Supply Chain). Use quick links to Feed, Risk, Elections.
  • Search: Top bar — choose type (Country, Region, Risk, etc.) and type to jump (e.g. workspace shortcuts). / or ⌘K focus.
  • Compare: Workspace — 2–4 panels. You choose any screen per panel (dropdown + path); Quick examples on the toolbar load sample layouts only.
  • Think tanks: See the Think tanks tab — citations, brief mode, shareable URLs, and briefing workflow.

Institutional models

GEPI, CDEI, SFI, Fragility power the Risk Engine, country dashboards, and article implications.

GEPI (Escalation Pressure Index)

Forward-looking pressure gauge. Channels: military, sanctions, rhetoric, domestic unrest, diplomacy breakdown, supply-chain stress. GEPI(t) = σ( Σ w_k · z_k(t) ). Daily global index 0–1.

CDEI (Chokepoint Disruption Exposure)

Per-country vulnerability to chokepoint disruptions. Flow share × dynamic risk × logistics penalty. Output: one number per country; per sector (energy, food, tech).

SFI (Sanctions Friction Index)

How hard sanctions bite. SFI(t) = Σ I_e · E_e · C_e — severity × enforceability × graph centrality.

Fragility (CFC-LSM)

Hidden state from observed indicators: food inflation, FX stress, protests, repression, elite splits, fiscal stress. Kalman/particle filter. Outputs: level, trend, jump risk.

GEG, TBCS, MLAT, Escalation Hazard

GEG: Typed causal event graph. TBCS: Treaty vs behavior alignment. MLAT: Multi-layer alignment (vote, trade, security, tech, narrative). Escalation Hazard: P(escalation within H days).

Think tanks & workflows

Geopolitical Terminal is built for monitor → triage → analyze → brief loops. Use the tools below for auditability and team handoff.

Every page

  • As of & feed time — Top context strip shows UTC “as of” and last feed ingestion (where available).
  • Copy page link — Captures the full URL including filters and query params for shareable workspace state.
  • Brief mode — Larger type and less chrome for reading or presenting; toggled from the strip.
  • Method & data limits — Expandable note on sources, models, and verification expectations.
  • On first visit — Optional role picker (stored only in your browser) adjusts emphasis; dismiss with Skip.

Citations & references

  • Articles, digests, and country dashboards show a stable GN-… reference and Copy citation (APA-style line with URL and access date).
  • Exports (e.g. briefing PDF) include a footer disclaimer; use Confidential cover options in the builder when needed.

Collaboration & outputs

  • Saved views — Persist filter sets; combine with “Copy page link” for colleagues.
  • Briefing builder — PDF/DOCX packs for meetings; article pages link in with one click.
  • Sensitivity & legal review — In the briefing builder, set export labels (public / internal / restricted) and “legal review required”; defaults live under Account. PDFs and DOCX headers show the label.

  • Discussion threads — On articles, digests, and country dashboards, logged-in users can post threaded comments tied to that page (not the same as Messaging channels).
  • Change history — Articles log text updates on re-ingest; saved briefings log edits. “Updated since your last visit” uses your last view timestamp.
  • Intelligence Messaging — Drag items from pages into channels for team discussion.
  • Workspace — Multi-panel layouts; share the URL to replicate layout.

Role-based navigation

If you picked a role in the first-run dialog, the app may simplify the top nav (e.g. Comms hides Security & Politics groups; Monitor hides Scenario Engine under Tools). Stored only in your browser.

Trust & limitations

Scores and LLM synthesis are indicative. They are not forecasts of government action, legal outcomes, or markets. Always corroborate with primary documents and institutional process.
